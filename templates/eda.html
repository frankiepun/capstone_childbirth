EDA (Exploratory Data Analysis)

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) is a crucial step in any machine learning project, as it allows us to gain a deep understanding of the data we are working with. By analyzing and visualizing the distribution, relationships, and patterns within the data, we can identify potential issues and biases, determine the relevant features, and decide on appropriate pre-processing techniques. EDA also helps us to make informed decisions about the appropriate modeling approach to take, and to fine-tune hyperparameters for better model performance. Additionally, EDA can help us to communicate our findings to stakeholders, providing valuable insights and building trust in the results. In short, EDA is a vital step in any machine learning project, as it sets the foundation for the entire data analysis process.

Data Source
Data source is a critical aspect of EDA. The quality and quantity of the data we use directly affect the accuracy and validity of the insights we gain from EDA and the accuracy of the model. We plan to use CDC (Center of Disease Control) dataset for this project. The CDC childbirth dataset contains detailed information on births in the US, including demographic information on mothers and fathers, characteristics of the pregnancy and delivery, and information on the birth outcomes.

We use CDC 2021 birth data which includes 99.94% of 2021 childbirths in the US, except the US territories. It has over 3.6 million records, and the file size is over 4GB. Out of the 590 available features, we selected 79 features for EDA, based on interviews with subject matter experts such as OBGYN doctors, midwives, and pediatricians.
